Mural Nomad

Mural Nomad is a project I built from start to finish. Having found inspiration by women in the arts who work with textiles, drawing artist, graphic designers, architects and interior designers, I started Mural Nomad.
Mural Nomad are my personal hand drawn works printed using the process of dye sublimation to print on a silk/polyster blend at big sizes.
I created a logo and using Shopify's free themes I was able to design an e-commerce site to sell my tapestries and drawing work.

Change My View

A personal favorite, Change My View Reader was built based on the popular SubReddit page "Change My View" where we built a Mobile-First reader of views and opinions using RESTful API's to fetch the information which we organized and displayed in a fresh way to show the different sides of the original project. Search the content of the site to find opinions from "cheese" to "politics" topics to read and maybe change your mind about.

Sharing Space

A mobile-first application to book unique office spaces around Austin Texas. I built this with a team while at DevBootcamp; an intense web development program focused on Agile Git workflow and accomplishing goals on very tight deadlines while learning new skills. Pairing with other Engineers to explore programming challenges and daily group projects that involved languages like Ruby, Javascript, HTML, CSS, Node.js and using tools like jQuery, Ajax, ActiveRecord, SQL, Angular, API’s and testing using RSpec and Jasmine.

Goteya Logo

Goteya is a brand based in Brooklyn, New York, and is owned and operated by women and immigrants.
Their aim is "to provide fashion-forward and accessible eye-wear to people around the world." Goteya asked me to create a new logo for the online store and I was inspired by their focus on accessibility and affordability. Goteya borrows its name from Joteya, the off-brand section of a Moroccan souk (our founder's country of birth).
